title: "Skyscraper Live: Alex Honnold vs. Taipei 101"
---

Alex Honnold just free soloed Taipei 101.

No ropes, no harness. 1,667 feet of glass and steel. He topped out in under 92 minutes, and I watched the whole thing essentially holding my breath. I climb at my local crag on weekends — nothing serious — but that context made it worse, not better. I know just enough to understand how wrong any single moment could go.

### The Technical Beta: Steel vs. Granite

Rock climbing on granite is hard. This looks nothing like that. There's no texture to read, no crack systems, no features your hands instinctively know what to do with. The whole building is just... smooth and uniform and unforgiving.

**The Slabs (0–100m):** The opening section has these *Ruyi* flared geometries — decorative architectural details that Honnold apparently spent months figuring out how to use as holds. Watching him move through it was strange. He'd pause in places that looked completely blank and then just... go. Finding edges in gaps most people would walk past without a second glance.

**The Bamboo Boxes (100–450m):** This is the bulk of the climb — eight stacked "bamboo boxes" that make up the tower's midsection. Each box is tilted outward at 7–15°, so he wasn't just going up, he was fighting the angle on every move. At the top of each section there's a "Dragon" geometry that flares wide, requiring serious core tension to stay on the wall. Hundreds of meters of that. I can't imagine the forearm pump.

**The Towers & Spire (450m+):** Once he hit the observation deck, the wind picked up visibly on the live stream. The transition to the spire — just exposed steel above the clouds — felt like the whole event narrowed to a single point. Nothing between him and a very long fall.

> "The challenge is the overall physicality. I don't know how it's going to feel until I'm on it."
>
> — **Alex Honnold**

### After

I've been thinking about it since he topped out. Not in some grand philosophical way — just the specific image of him standing up there, above Taipei, completely alone. It looked quiet in a way that felt earned.

I'll be back at the crag next weekend, projecting the same V4 I've been working on for a month. Different world. But I get it a little more now.
